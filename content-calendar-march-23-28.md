## TUESDAY, March 24 — Educational Tip: Roof Inspection

**Image:** `roofing-overhead.jpg` (from website)

### Facebook Caption:
Quick tip for Eastern Panhandle homeowners — you don't need to climb a ladder to check your roof after winter.

Grab a pair of binoculars and look for these three things from the ground:

1. Missing or lifting shingles (wind damage is common after this winter)
2. Dark granules piling up in your gutters (means your shingles are losing their weatherproofing)
3. Bent or rusted flashing around your chimney and vent pipes (spring rain will find its way in)

If your roof is over 15 years old, it's worth getting a professional inspection. Insurance carriers in WV, VA, and MD are getting stricter about roof age in 2026.

We do free roof assessments across Martinsburg, Charles Town, Inwood, and the surrounding area. Call (681) 534-5515 or visit realelitecontracting.com/contact.

## THURSDAY, March 26 — Educational Tip: Siding & Trim Check

**Image:** `siding-replacement.jpg` (from website)

### Facebook Caption:
Here's something most homeowners miss after winter — checking your siding and exterior trim for moisture damage.

Vinyl, wood, and fiber cement siding all expand and contract during freezing temperatures. Once the snow melts, small gaps can let moisture in behind the panels. Here's what to look for:

Warped or buckled siding panels — this usually means moisture is trapped behind them.

Soft spots in wood trim — take a screwdriver and gently press into the trim around your windows and doors. If the wood feels spongy or gives way, you've got rot that needs to be cut out before it spreads to your framing.

Catching this early saves thousands. If you're not sure what you're looking at, we'll come take a look for free. Call (681) 534-5515 or visit realelitecontracting.com/contact.
