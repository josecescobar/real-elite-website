## The 2-Hour Response Window

Within **2 hours** of a confirmed tri-state hail or wind event:

1. ☐ **Confirm storm path.** Pull NWS warning ZIPs from weather.gov + NOAA SPC storm reports. Identify the 3–5 affected ZIPs.
2. ☐ **Raise Google LSA bid +50%** in affected ZIPs.
3. ☐ **Launch geo-fenced Google Search ad** with the templates below. Daily budget $150–$300/affected ZIP for 72 hours.
4. ☐ **Post storm photos to Google Business Profile** within 2 hours of crew getting safe ground views.
5. ☐ **Post storm response Reel + carousel** to Meta (Facebook + Instagram).
6. ☐ **Send Birdeye / NiceJob blast SMS** to past customers in affected ZIPs offering free post-storm inspection.
7. ☐ **Order EDDM mailer drop** for affected ZIPs (target: hit mailboxes within 5 business days).

The window is real. Miss it and the lead economics flip from $45–$75 CPL to $110+/lead.

## Confirmed Storm Triggers

Trigger this playbook on any of the following NWS / NOAA conditions in Berkeley / Jefferson / Morgan WV, Washington / Frederick MD, or Clarke / Frederick / Loudoun VA:

- Severe Thunderstorm Warning with **hail ≥1 inch** (quarter-sized or larger)
- High Wind Warning with **gusts ≥58 mph**
- Tornado Warning (any rating)
- Winter Storm Warning with **ice accumulation ≥0.25 inch**
- Named tropical system with rainfall ≥3" in 24h

Sub-trigger (lower confidence, raise LSA bid only +25%, no paid mailer):
- Severe Thunderstorm Watch active for >4 hours covering target ZIPs

## Door-Knock Script (Optional Manual Lift)

When a homeowner books an inspection, knock the **3 nearest visible-roof homes** on the same street:

> "Hey, I'm with Real Elite Contracting. We're doing a free roof inspection at [neighbor's first name]'s place down the street after last night's storm — same crew, same offer, we can swing by yours too while we're already on the block. No charge, no obligation. Want me to text you a slot for this week?"

Why it works: high relevance (you're already in the neighborhood), high trust (named neighbor), low effort (text follow-up). Industry-standard close rate: 15–25% in storm-affected neighborhoods.

## Compliance Notes

- **No incentives for Google reviews.** Google bans this outright; listing can be suspended. (FTC Consumer Reviews Rule, Oct 2024, also applies broadly.)
- **No "fake before-and-after" creative in Meta ads.** If using AI tools to generate after-photos for marketing speculation, label it clearly or do not use it. Real after-photos only.
- **Insurance claim language:** never promise a specific claim outcome. Always say "we document damage for your insurance carrier" — never "we'll get your claim approved."
- **Storm chasing reputation risk:** lead with the veteran identity and the local 200+ project history. Do not impersonate insurance adjusters. Do not knock on doors past 8 PM.
