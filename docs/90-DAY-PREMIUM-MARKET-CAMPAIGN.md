# 90-Day Premium Market Campaign

## Campaign overview

**Campaign:** Planned Before Priced  
**Window:** 90 days from launch  
**Primary objective:** Generate 12 qualified consultation requests for $40,000+ kitchen, primary-suite, basement/addition, and covered outdoor-living projects in Loudoun, Jefferson, and Berkeley counties.  
**Secondary objective:** Turn at least four qualified consultations into in-home scope visits and establish cost-per-qualified-lead benchmarks before scaling paid media.

The campaign does not promise a revenue target before baseline conversion and close-rate data exist. “Qualified” means the service area, project type, planning band, and timing are plausible and the homeowner completes the premium consultation intake.

## Audience

### Primary: Loudoun design-led homeowner

Homeowners in Leesburg, Ashburn, Brambleton, Lansdowne, and Middleburg planning a substantial renovation. They value finish quality, communication, protection of the occupied home, and a contractor comfortable collaborating with a designer or architect.

### Secondary: Eastern Panhandle growth homeowner

Established and newer homeowners in Charles Town, Shepherdstown, Martinsburg, and Spring Mills who need more capable space: a finished lower level, addition, upgraded kitchen/suite, or complete outdoor-living plan.

### Main objections

- “Will the contractor communicate and protect the rest of the home?”
- “Is our budget realistic for the scope?”
- “Who is accountable after the sale?”
- “Can they coordinate design, selections, permits, and trades?”

## Message hierarchy

**Core message:** A major renovation should be planned before it is priced.

1. Start with a written brief and realistic planning band.
2. Work with one accountable project lead rather than a sales handoff.
3. Confirm fit by phone before scheduling an in-home appointment.
4. Use a veteran-owned, regional contractor licensed across VA, WV, and MD.

Proof must come from real project photography, written scope examples, verified reviews, and completed project stories. Do not use unsupported “best,” “top-rated,” margin, ROI, or market-dominance claims.

## Channel strategy

|---|---|---|
| Campaign landing page | Explain offers and qualify demand | Complete at launch |

| Email/SMS follow-up | Fast response and appointment confirmation | Low |
| HOA/referral partners | Neighborhood credibility and introductions | Medium |

Paid media remains off until form delivery, attribution, call response, and qualification reporting are confirmed. When funded, start 60–70% with high-intent search, reserve 15–20% for retargeting/tests, and keep 10–15% contingency.

## Measurement

| KPI | 90-day target | Source |
|---|---:|---|
| Qualified consultation requests | 12 | Lead ledger + intake fields |
| Qualified-to-in-home rate | 33%+ | CRM/lead ledger |
| Contact within four business hours | 90%+ | Lead timestamps |
| Landing-to-lead conversion | Establish baseline; target 2.5%+ after 200 sessions | GA4 `premium_campaign_view` and `generate_lead` |
| Proposal and close rates | Establish baseline | CRM/lead ledger |
| Cost per qualified lead | Set only after media budget is approved | Ad platform spend / qualified leads |

Review weekly for response failures and bad traffic; review the business decision at days 30, 60, and 90. Do not optimize against raw form volume alone.

## UTM convention

Use lowercase values and keep the campaign constant:

`utm_campaign=premium_market_90_day`

Examples:

- `utm_source=google&utm_medium=cpc&utm_campaign=premium_market_90_day&utm_content=kitchen`
- `utm_source=google_business&utm_medium=organic&utm_campaign=premium_market_90_day&utm_content=outdoor`
- `utm_source=hoa_partner&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=premium_market_90_day&utm_content=partner_name`

## Risks and controls

1. **Planning bands do not match actual delivery economics.** Owner reviews the ranges against job-cost history before paid promotion; adjust after five qualified calls.
2. **Premium language outruns proof.** Use only real work and verified reviews; label inspiration imagery clearly elsewhere on the site.
3. **Lead response is slow.** Assign one owner, timestamp every lead, and audit the four-business-hour standard weekly.
4. **Paid traffic produces low-fit repair inquiries.** Use service, location, and planning-band negatives; evaluate qualified CPL rather than raw CPL.
