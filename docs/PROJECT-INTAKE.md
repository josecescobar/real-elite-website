# Project Intake — what to send so we can build your project stories

This is the single most valuable thing you can do to move the site forward. Every finished job you
send becomes a **project story page** that then shows up automatically on the gallery, the matching
service page (e.g. a roofing job appears on the Roofing page), the matching city page, and — if the
customer left a review — the Review Center. Author once, it works everywhere, forever.

**Goal: 10–12 past jobs.** A good mix: 2–3 roofing, 1–2 decks, 1–2 kitchens, 1 bathroom, 1 basement,
1 siding/exterior, and 1 paving job. It's fine to send them a few at a time — don't wait to have all
12 perfect.

You don't need to write anything polished. Bullet points, a voice memo, or a quick text is plenty —
we'll turn it into the finished page. **Just don't make anything up:** real photos, real jobs, real
numbers. If you don't know a detail, leave it blank.

## For each job, send:

**1. Photos** (the most important part — the site's quality is set by the photos)
- 5–15 photos per job. A mix is ideal: a few "before," a few during the work, and the best "after" shots.
- Phone photos are fine. Landscape (sideways) shots usually look best on the site.
- Drop them in a folder named for the job, or just text/email them grouped by job.

**2. A few facts** (whatever you know)
- **What it was:** e.g. "full roof replacement," "composite deck build," "kitchen gut remodel"
- **Where:** the town (e.g. Martinsburg, Charles Town, Frederick, Leesburg) — street address not needed and won't be published
- **Roughly what it cost:** a range is perfect (e.g. "around $18k") — we publish only an anonymized band like "$15k–$20k," never the exact price
- **How long it took:** e.g. "3 days," "about 5 weeks"
- **Materials/brands used:** e.g. "GAF Timberline shingles," "Trex decking" (helps you show up in searches)

**3. The story in a sentence or two each** (this is what makes the page sell)
- **What the homeowner wanted / their problem:** e.g. "old roof was leaking, wanted it done before winter"
- **What made it tricky, if anything:** e.g. "1920s house, had to match the historic look" or "found rotted decking once we tore off" — this is where your expertise shows
- **How it turned out:** one line on the result

**4. A review, if you have one**
- If the customer said something nice (text, email, Google review, or just told you), send it word-for-word plus their first name + last initial and town (e.g. "Mike & Sarah T., Martinsburg"). We never publish full names or contact info.

## Example (this level of detail is plenty)

> **Job: deck build, Charles Town**
> Photos: [12 attached]
> Composite deck, ~$22k, took about 2 weeks. Trex Transcend decking, aluminum railings.
> They had an old rotting wood deck they were scared to walk on, wanted low-maintenance. Tricky part
> was a big grade drop off the back — had to step it down in two levels. Came out great.
> Review: "The craftsmanship is outstanding — every detail was perfect." — Jennifer R., Charles Town

That's it. Send what you've got and we'll build the pages.
